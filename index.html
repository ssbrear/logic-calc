Logic Calculator
This page will attempt to discern the validity of a logical syllogism. A syllogism is a kind of logical argument that applies deductive reasoning to arrive at a conclusion based on two or more propositions. No distinction will be made between a major and a minor premise, and only two premises will be considered for any given argument.
Example:
1. All dogs are cute
2. Biscuit is a dog
3. Therefore, Biscuit is cute

This argument is valid, regardless of whether or not there exists a dog named Biscuit, and whether or not Biscuit is in fact, cute.

As can be seen in the image on the left, the first premise requires that all dogs be a subset of the "Cute" category, and since Biscuit is defined as a dog in premise 2, that necessitates that Biscuit be cute.

Note: The is/are selector is purely for grammatical purposes and has no effect on the conclusion.
